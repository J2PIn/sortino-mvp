Methodology

Sortino ranks agencies using a mix of public signals and verified performance evidence. The goal is transparency: each rank is designed to be explainable, freshness-aware, and resistant to gaming.

MVP notice

This is version v1. Data coverage and safeguards will expand. We will publish methodology updates and keep an audit trail of major changes.

What we measure

Sortino computes an overall score from a set of category scores. In the MVP, categories include:

Proof of Performance
Structured case studies and results, weighted by verification and recency.
Customer Satisfaction
Review rating, review volume, and recency; with anomaly checks.
Credibility
Awards and recognitions; weighted by significance and decaying over time.
Market Presence
Directional demand signals such as website activity and trend.
Authority & Activity
Publishing cadence and consistency (with diminishing returns).
Social Proof
Social presence and trend; designed to reduce the impact of inflated counts.

Where the data comes from

Public signals are collected from public sources (for example: awards listings, review pages, publishing activity, social profiles, and directional demand estimates).

Agency-submitted evidence is optional. Agencies can submit anonymized case studies (industry theme + channel + timeframe + baseline → outcome). Supporting evidence can be uploaded in a redacted form.

Note: some public metrics are inherently noisy or estimated. We treat these as directional signals and weight them accordingly.

Verification & confidence

Not all datapoints are treated equally. Each metric is assigned a confidence weight based on:

  • Source reliability (public vs submitted; platform vs estimate)
  • Freshness (recent datapoints contribute more than stale ones)
  • Verification status (unverified vs evidence-uploaded vs verified)
  • Anti-gaming checks (confidence can be reduced if patterns look abnormal)
Verification levels
Unverified: claimed or collected without supporting evidence.
Evidence uploaded: supporting materials provided (redacted).
Verified: evidence reviewed and consistency-checked.
Disputed: flagged for review; contribution reduced or removed.

Freshness & update frequency

Rankings are recalculated regularly. To reduce noise, Sortino emphasizes trends and applies short-window smoothing rather than allowing small daily changes to cause major rank swings.

Older datapoints decay in influence over time. For example, new reviews or newly verified results can move an agency more than content published years ago.

Normalization & weighting

Metrics are normalized so agencies can be compared on a common scale (0–100). Category scores are then combined into an overall score using fixed weights.

We use techniques like outlier capping and diminishing returns for easily inflated metrics (e.g., followers or content volume).

Anti-gaming safeguards

Sortino applies safeguards to reduce manipulation and reward verifiable performance. Examples include:

  • Diminishing returns on easily inflated metrics (e.g., follower counts, post volume)
  • Confidence penalties for abnormal spikes (e.g., sudden review surges or follower jumps)
  • Reduced weight for unverified outlier performance claims
  • Audit trails for verification and disputes
Important

Sortino does not sell ranking positions. Paid programs cover verification work, analytics, and optional features — rankings follow the same scoring rules for everyone.

Corrections & disputes

Agencies and users can request corrections if data appears inaccurate. If a datapoint is disputed or found invalid, its confidence is reduced or removed, and the audit trail is preserved.
